About HOJ Bible Resource

Our Beliefs

The House of Jacob Bible Study Resource is for those who seek entrance into the Kingdom of God. It is a place for those who wish to recognize and learn about all that is written in the complete Holy Bible to congregate and learn. We offer a space for those who have faith in the testimony of Jesus Christ (Matthew 22: 34-40) and observe all of God’s laws accordingly, including: the Ten commandments (Exodus 20:1-17), God’s Dietary Law (Leviticus 11) and God’s Feast Days (Leviticus 23). We also acknowledge and teach the lineage of all families of the earth including the Gentiles, Hamites and Shemites (Genesis 10). This includes the belief that Israelite is a physical lineage coming from Abraham, Isaac, and Jacob (Genesis 35: 10-13)as well a spiritual desegnation comprising of a mixed multitude who seve the God of the Bible (Exodus 12:35-41 & I Corinthians 10:1-4). We believe that the global suffering of the African Diaspora, who were historically taken on slave ships into various countries, is the result of them being the physical seed of Israel who have turned away from the Lord God of Israel (Deuteronomy 28 & 2 Kings 17: 9-24 & Luke 3:1-2 & Luke 21:24). We believe that God created a natural order (Exodus 19: 3-6 & Act 10) that commands the true descendants of the Israelites should serve te God of Israel and teach the whole world how to achieve salvation, which is open to all people to desire to serve God according to His commandments (Titus 2:11-13 & Isaiah 56:5-7).

Statement of Purpuse

This Bible Study Resource is meant to offer a central, online location to learn about the true and living God, the God of Abraham, Isaac, and Jacob, our messiah, and high priest Jesus Christ. Our free resources include a copy of the authorized KJV with commentary written to explain difficult and little known themes in the bible, audio, video, and pdf copies of biblical lessons, a page where common biblical questions can be asked and addressed, and a locating service which uses your geolocation to suggest a church to attend and congregate on the Sabbath.
